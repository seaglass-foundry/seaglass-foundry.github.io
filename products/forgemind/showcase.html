What you are looking at

This walks through a single recorded session, S-MARATHON-22 — a generated test scenario that simulates a combat search-and-rescue recovery being planned through ForgeMind™. It is not a record of a real operation. It is a deliberately demanding evaluation, run end to end against real SRTM terrain in the Galiuro Mountains of southeast Arizona. One hundred and two operations, requested in plain English, from “mark the survivor's last known position” through the final “list everything on the map so I can confirm the recovery package is complete.” Every one was understood, type-checked, routed, and executed. All 102 succeeded.

Why a marathon, and why this one matters

Most demos show a system answering one clever question. That proves very little. Real operational work is not one question — it is a long chain of dependent decisions where each step relies on the exact entities, geometry, and assumptions established in the steps before it. The interesting question is not “can it answer a prompt” but “can it hold a coherent operational picture across a hundred linked requests without drifting, hallucinating, or quietly inventing an answer.”

S-MARATHON-22 is that test. It models a punishing scenario — the kind of plan where a wrong elevation, a misresolved waypoint, or a route that clips a threat ring would be the difference between a recovered survivor and a downed aircraft. The session was run end to end against real terrain, and it held.

“List everything on the map so I can confirm the recovery package is complete.” — Operation 102. By this point ForgeMind is tracking 29 named entities created across the previous 101 operations, each still addressable by name.

Read the plan, not just the pass rate

The numbers are internally consistent in a way a guessing system could not fake.

hlz_charlie is the only one of three landing-zone candidates that sits outside the MANPADS weapon-engagement zone (op 43), and it also has the flattest ground at 1.12° (op 37). The direct ingress route crosses the threat ring (op 54), so the least-cost path is longer at 10.99 km but masked. The overwatch position can see the survivor (op 69) but is not skylined to the MANPADS site (op 71). This is a coherent, self-consistent recovery plan, computed from real elevation data.

Why it does not get things confidently wrong

A normal LLM agent loop would try to be the GIS engine — reason about coordinates, guess at operations, and emit an answer that sounds right. That is exactly where confident-wrong results come from. ForgeMind is built the opposite way.
